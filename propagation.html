Propagating Succulents

How to Propagate Succulents from Cuttings and Leaves

Step 1

Succulents kept in small pots require frequent maintenance, particularly if they do not receive enough light. Insufficient sunlight causes the plant to become leggy like this. These plants are perfect for propagation!

Step 2

Although this plant still looks normal from the top, the lower leaves will gradually wither and fall off, leaving a long stem with a rosette high above the soil. Propagation provides an opportunity to make use of these leaves before they start to die.

Step 3

Start by removing the lower leaves. Be careful when you remove the leaves from the stem to ensure a clean break. Hold the leaf gently and wiggle it from side to side until I feel a little snap. If you tear the leaf or leave the base still attached to the stem, it will not be able to grow roots or a new plant.

Step 4

This is what the leaves will look like if they are pulled cleanly from the stem, remaining fully intact.

Step 5

After you have successfully removed the lower leaves you will be left with a small rosette on a long bare stem.

Step 6

I use a pair of craft scissors, but a sharp knife would work great as well. Cut the stem about an inch below the rosette. Make sure your cut is as straight as possible.

Step 7

Now you should have a rosette with a short stem and a long bare stem with no rosette. Don’t throw that away! We’ll use that too.

Step 8

Let the cut ends of your rosette and stem dry out for a few days. This is called callousing over and it helps to prevent rot.

Once you feel like the ends are dry enough, you can place them on top of some well-draining cactus or succulent soil. (Some people dip the ends in a rooting hormone, but I’ve never tried this and have had great success without it.) I’ve had the best results when keeping my leaves indoors in a window with lots of indirect sunlight.

Step 9

After a few weeks you will see little pink roots sprouting from the ends of the leaves and then teeny tiny baby plants will begin to grow. I water the leaves very rarely until I start to see roots or babies appearing. I like to use a mix of cactus soil and perlite.

Step 10

Water your cuttings sparingly, allowing the soil to dry out completely between waterings. In a few weeks, you should see new growth, and eventually, your cuttings will grow into full-sized plants! At this point I will give the roots or baby plants a good soak about once a week or whenever I notice the soil is totally dry. Just like with a fully developed succulent, too much water is not good. If you want to be certain not to over water, I recommend spraying the ends of your leaves with a spray bottle once a day instead of completely soaking the soil.

Step 11

I let my baby plants grow in rows like this until I start to notice the “mother leaf” starting to wither. At that point I will carefully remove the leaf in the same fashion I removed it from the original stem, and then place the baby plant in its own pot. This can be tricky as you don’t want to accidentally remove the roots with the leaf, so you may want to play it safe and let the leaf fall off on its own.

Step 12

And here are the new plants that grew from the propagated leaves and stem. Succulent propagation is a great way to get more plants for free!

Step 13

Here is another example of a leggy succulent that was propagated. You can see the new growth at the base of the plant where the old leaves were removed.

Step 14

Instead of using individual pots with this batch, I just removed the leaves as they withered and left the plants to grow together like a little succulent forest.

Step 15

Back to that stump. No part of this plant is going to go to waste!

Simply put this pot aside and eventually it will begin to sprout new plants from each place we removed a leaf!

Step 16

Now back to our original plant, the reason we did all this! Once the stem has dried out and calloused over, simply place your plant back in a pot with well-draining cactus or succulent soil and it will grow roots again and continue to flourish!
